Additional info regarding the work logic and development of the analyses.
& other work-in-progress projects.

Doing financial-statement analyses is not exact science, but of a logical art and craft. I attempted to go into this analysis with the best of my own intuition. However, not everything came out perfect and to an exact answer like given course materials. The difficulties that arose when general analysis practices did not work well with Boeing. Boeing relied on investments, short and long term to cover some operating expenses, meaning the company doesn't pull from their revolving credit. This is quite important when dealing with the cash plug for balancing the statements. Another variable that made forecasting difficult was the company decision to stop stock repurchases and dividends entirely. This added another layer of difficulty navigating the forecasting process. Overall, my analysis of Boeing is far from perfect, but it perfectly highlights how much of it is a craft. For now, this project will be an ongoing-project for continued improvement.

In addition to financial analysis, I have taken interests in coding langauges. As of right now, I am learning C++ to eventually make fun software projects, as well as productivity projects in the future. It may seem unrelated; however, I do see many synergies with programming and financial work as it can automate some workload. As for now, this is also a work-in-progress.
